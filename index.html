Michael Betancourt, PhD
Postdoctoral Research Associate
Department of Statistics
University of Warwick
I'm a once and future physicist currently masquerading as a statistician. My work bridges theory and methodology in order to develop the general purpose statistical tools and pedagogical resources that enable scientists to make the most out of their data.

This pursuit has lead me to the application of differential geometry to Markov Chain Monte Carlo techniques, in particulr Hamiltonian Monte Carlo. Along with some talented colleagues I am developing both the theoretical foundations and practical implementations of the algorithm, the latter particularly in Stan.
